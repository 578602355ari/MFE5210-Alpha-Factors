# Reference for MFE5210 Alpha Factors Project

1. BaoStock API Documentation. (2025). Historical A-share daily price and volume data query. 
   Available at: http://baostock.com

2. Jegadeesh, N., & Titman, S. (1993). 
   Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers: Implications for Stock Market Efficiency. 
   Journal of Finance, 48(1), 65–91. 
   - Source of the momentum factor (F1_MOM5, F4_RANK_MOM) construction logic.

3. Lee, C. M. C., & Swaminathan, B. (2000). 
   Price Momentum and Trading Volume. 
   Journal of Finance, 55(5), 2017–2069. 
   - Basis for volume spike and volume change factors (F2_VOL_SPIKE, F3_VOL_CHANGE).

4. Datar, V. T., Naik, N. Y., & Radcliffe, R. (1998). 
   Liquidity and stock returns: An alternative test. 
   Journal of Financial Markets, 1(2), 203–219. 
   - Reference for using volume-based volatility (F5_VOL_VOLATILITY) as a cross-sectional factor.

5. Quantitative Investment Industry Standard. 
   Cross-sectional factor preprocessing: Winsorization at 1%/99% and daily standardization. 
   Common practice in A-share quantitative research.

6. Quantitative Factor Evaluation Framework. 
   Spearman rank correlation (IC) and IC Sharpe ratio calculation. 
   Standard method for evaluating predictive power of alpha factors in academic and industry practice.
